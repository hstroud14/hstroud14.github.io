Physics Festival Demo

The first weekend in April marks the annual Physics and Engineering Festival at Texas A&M. Hannah has been involved with the festival since her freshman year of undergrad, when she operated the demonstration on Eddy currents, and has been a fan ever since. She has participated with her lab group in recent years, demonstrating how shape memory alloys work.

For the 2019 festival, Hannah designed, built, and operated a two part demo to explain the RED concept in a hands-on activity. With the help of undergrad Collin Invie, Hannah constructed a demo designed to show interactively how inserting a medical device in a human body can be similar to docking a spacecraft in space.

The medical device demo allowed students to navigate a guide wire through a "blood vessel" (clear tubing) to an occluded artery where they simulated deploying a stent with an inflating balloon. The sister demo simulated a spacecraft docking with another craft. A robotic arm rotated on a turn-table and was maneuvered into a specific position within the booster of the other craft to prepare to dock. A balloon held by the robotic arm was inflated to join the two crafts.
