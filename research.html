Current Projects

Multiphysical Modeling of Degrading Structures Under Flow (2020-Present)

Structural elements, when exposed to fluid flow, can often lose material due to natural processes such as corrosion and erosion. In biomedical engineering, some devices, known as bioabsorbable devices, rely on such degradation to fulfill their purpose. The complex interactions between fluid and structure that result in material loss are difficult to understand, however. Hannah's work seeks to develop and couple a material model and fluid-structure interaction model to predict how structures of varying geometries will degrade given certain flow conditions.

Introducing Aerospace Concepts to K-12 Students Through an Online Game (2020-Present)

Students are constantly learning through relating concepts to their own experiences. To that end, Hannah and her team developed an interactive online game designed to introduce concepts of space traffic management, an important field in aerospace engineering, through road traffic parallels. Past Projects

Torque Tube Modeling (2019-2020)

Shape memory alloy torque tubes are a geometry of SMAs that allow for compact torsional actuation or energy dissipation. Hannah has modeled the response of torsional actuators under different loading and power conditions in the FEA software ABAQUS. She wrote a review paper on SMA torque tubes and their applications and capabilities.

SMA Knitted Structures (2018-2020)

Shape memory alloys have long been studied in biomedical and aerospace research; the biomedical industry often uses SMA because of its ability to handle large non-permanent deformations (catheterization of stents), while the aerospace industry often employs SMAs as actuators. A structure like a knit allows SMA devices to be more flexible, a useful feature for implanted biomedical devices that must move with the body's natural motions. It also allows for a highly tailorable actuator response. Hannah's work involved modeling the response of SMA knitted structures using the Lagoudas and coworkers SMA model and the FEA software ABAQUS.

Avian Wing (2016-2018)

The avian-inspired, shape memory alloy actuated, camber morphing wing employs skin-based SMA actuators to convert an airfoil from a cruise configuration to a highly cambered, high lift configuration. SMA wires embedded in a PDMS matrix form a composite actuator that, when secured to the lower surface of the wing, shorten that surface and pull the trailing edge down. Hannah's primary role on this project was in physical design/CAD modeling and manufacturing. She developed, manufactured, and wind tunnel tested both a 3D printed static model and a fibreglass-epoxy composite morphing model.

Deployable Solar Array (2015)

The deployable solar array uses an SMA torque tube powered with less than 50W to convert a series of solar panels from a compact, "stored" configuration to an open configuration. Hannah's work on this project included mechanism design and Matlab modeling of the torque tube heating cycle. Hannah and her team placed 1st in the first CASMART design copetition with this design.
