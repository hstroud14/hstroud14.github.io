Current Projects

Torque Tube Modeling (2019-Present)

Shape memory alloy torque tubes are a geometry of SMAs that allow for compact torsional actuation or energy dissipation. Hannah models the response of torsional actuators under different loading and power conditions.

SMA Knitted Structures (2018-Present)

Shape memory alloys have long been studied in biomedical and aerospace research; the biomedical industry often uses SMA because of its ability to handle large non-permanent deformations (catheterization of stents), while the aerospace industry often employs SMAs as actuators. A non-linear structure like a knit allows SMA devices to be more flexible, a useful feature for implanted biomedical devices that must move with the body's natural motions. It also allows for a highly tailorable actuator response. Hannah's work involves modeling the non-linear response of SMA knitted structures using the Lagoudas SMA model and the structural modeling software Abaqus.

Non-Conventional Aerospace Applications (2018-Present)

Hannah's work in non-traditional aerospace applications stems from a grant dedicated to organically increasing diversity in aerospace engineering by introducing students to less visible applications of their aerospace knowledge than airplanes, rockets, or spacecraft. Because aerospace engineering strongly focuses on fluids, structures, dynamics, and the intersections between these fields, problems that exist in this very "aerospace" arena often arise in fields such as biomedical, petroleum, and civil engineering. Hannah created a design elective for aerospace engineering seniors that emphasized these types of problems; students were tasked with designing smart inhalers, arterial stents, and improved tourniquets. This work is continued in the form of a two part senior capstone design scheduled to start Fall 2019.

Past Projects

Avian Wing (2016-2018)

The avian-inspired, shape memory alloy actuated, camber morphing wing employs skin-based SMA actuators to convert an airfoil from a cruise configuration to a highly cambered, high lift configuration. SMA wires embedded in a PDMS matrix form a composite actuator that, when secured to the lower surface of the wing, shorten that surface and pull the trailing edge down. Hannah's primary role on this project was in physical design/CAD modeling and manufacturing. She developed, manufactured, and wind tunnel tested both a 3D printed static model and a fibreglass-epoxy composite morphing model.

Deployable Solar Array (2015)

The deployable solar array uses an SMA torque tube powered with less than 50W to convert a series of solar panels from a compact, "stored" configuration to an open configuration. Hannah's work on this project included mechanism design and Matlab modeling of the torque tube heating cycle. Hannah and her team placed 1st in the first CASMART design copetition with this design.
