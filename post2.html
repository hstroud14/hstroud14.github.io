Camp SOAR

This summer, Hannah served as a camp counselor for Camp SOAR, a summer camp for high schoolers interested in aerospace engineering. This was a unique opportunity to come full circle, since Camp SOAR was the reason Hannah initially heard about Texas A&M in the first place (she attended the camp in 2013).

In addition to serving as a day counselor, Hannah also designed and directed an activity in her research area of non-conventional applications of aerospace. The activity required students to use their engineering skills and aerospace intuition through designing and building a delivery pod for medical equipment delivered via UAV drop. Students had to design a pod to protect their payload of syringes (glowsticks) from breaking as they fell from a 60 ft drop. They had one design session to plan and order the materials they believed they would need, and a build session the next day to construct their design. Students leanred about the importance of planning, good estimation, and adapting to problems by interacting with the engineering design process in a hands-on way.
