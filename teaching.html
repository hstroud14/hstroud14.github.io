Newly Developed Courses

K-12 Outreach Design (In Progress)

Hannah's current course development/teaching efforts are focused around a class designed to give undergraduate students the skills and hands-on experience to take their aerospace knowledge to their communities. The goal of this class will be to engage undergraduates in developing their own activities to perform with K-12 students.

Unconventional Aerospace Applications (2018-Present)

Hannah's work in non-traditional aerospace applications stems from a grant dedicated to organically increasing diversity in aerospace engineering by introducing students to less visible applications of their aerospace knowledge than airplanes, rockets, or spacecraft. Because aerospace engineering strongly focuses on fluids, structures, dynamics, and the intersections between these fields, problems that exist in this very "aerospace" arena often arise in fields such as biomedical, petroleum, and civil engineering. Hannah created a design elective for aerospace engineering seniors that emphasized these types of problems; students were tasked with designing smart inhalers, arterial stents, and improved tourniquets. She developed the coursework and activities, as well as served as TA and grader. This work continued in the form of a two part senior capstone design in Fall 2019, where an "unconventional" track was included.
